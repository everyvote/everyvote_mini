Constitution

-All EV software is open source (AGPLv3).

-All EV non-software artifacts are open source (CC BY-SA).

-EV will seek USA non-profit status (but does not yet have non-profit status).

-EV will never have advertisements.

-EV will seek donations and grants to pay expenses.

-EV will be 100% financially transparent. All expenses by EV must be made public information as soon as practically possible. All donors' names and the amount of their donations will be public information. If a donor makes an anonymous donation to EV, the identity of the donor should remain anonymous to EV as well as to the public.

-In order to prevent a monopoly on #opengov tools from forming, EV actively strives to make all of its software fully federation-compatible with as many other civic engagement technologies as possible. (For more information, please check out OpenGov Federation.)

-EV actively supports the development of free, publicly downloadable, comprehensive USA candidate and official databases, so that Americans can learn about, share their opinion, and contact all of their candidates and officials through one convenient web page.

-EV will never sell or share private user data with 3rd parties, unless EV finds evidence a user's EV account is being used to perpetuate indisputable abuse to other people, in which case EV may share that user's data with US law enforcement (this definition deserves more detail and public debate).

-EV is an unbiased, non-partisan organization that should not declare opinions on government policy, unless the policy may interfere or assist with the successful implementation of EV technology.

-EV can express support for the protection of self-evident, inalienable 21st century human rights for all people, including but not limited to: food, clean water, shelter, medicine, electricity, internet access, equal rights, and freedom of speech.

Goals

1) Establish a reliable, comprehensive, United States candidate and official database, that anyone can freely integrate into their own website. This database would list the names and basic identifying information (like party, contact info) of all of your officially registered candidates for every election you are eligible to vote in, including federal, state, county, township, local, metropolitan water district, school district, and every other elected office that American tax dollars support. It would also provide the names and contact information for all judges you are eligible to cast a retention vote on.

2) Help create and participate in a federated opinion sharing platform (OSP) network. Today, if you vote to Support a bill on OpenCongress, you need to resubmit that same vote if you later use a site like POPVOX. If you declare on ISideWith that you are Pro-Life and in favor of withdrawing troops from Afghanistan, you will have to resubmit those same stances again if you later use LoveGov. It would save you time and energy if you could submit these decisions and all of your other profile information just one time, and then have the option of automatically transferring your data to any other site that tracks the same information. That's exactly what OSP federation would allow you to do. In order to make OSP federation possible, OSPs like OpenCongress, POPVOX, ISideWith, LoveGov, and the proposed EveryVote.org platform would need to agree to identical open standards, which would ensure our data is transferable and cross-compatible. (For more information on this goal, please visit the OpenGov Federation page.)

#OpenGovFed

Network federation is the ability to transfer your data from one web site over to another in a compatible format. If two websites are truly federation-compatible, then any information you submit to one site could be automatically transferred to or synced with the other. Any number of websites can be federation-compatible.

Only network federation can prevent social network monopolies.

Without true network federation, social networks will always form into monopolies. Would you rather spend hours of your time developing a profile on a social network with 500 users, or a profile on one with 500,000,000 users? Naturally we choose the 1 or 2 websites that will put us in contact with the most people, and those websites rapidly form into monopolies too big for anyone to compete with. Once we're all inside, the websites lock their doors and hold us hostage to their policies, their politics, and their advertisements.

With network federation, social networks cannot form into monopolies. Say you create a profile and build a friends list on one website, but then a new website comes out that you like more. If the sites are federation-compatible, you could automatically transfer your profile and friends list to the new website. You could transfer all of your data from the old site to the new site as easily and quickly as possible. You could even sync your data on both sites, so a profile change on one site would automatically change your profile on the other, and a status update you post on one site would automatically post to the other.

With network federation, none of us would be trapped into using a social network just because someone invented it before everyone else. We could use as many social networks as we want, all at the same time, and more conveniently than ever. We could experiment with and use a different social network every week, transferring all of our data each time, and never losing our data in the process.

The vast majority of US #OpenGov tools are social networks, but they are social networks that no-one has a clear monopoly over ---yet.

Now ---before an #OpenGov monopoly forms--- is the best time for #OpenGov leaders and developers to collaborate and cooperate to make their tools federation-compatible, and begin participating in true network federation.. If we wait, then an #OpenGov monopoly will eventually form.

Budget

Current Funds: $0.00

EveryVote is a 100% financially transparent organization. All EveryVote incomes and expenses will be publicly available on this Budget page.

EveryVote currently has no funding and no paid staff. We are volunteers, but if enough funding becomes available, EveryVote can hire full-time staff to develop EveryVote software and manage organizational operations.
